How Studio Hero Structures Your Work

Every production your studio takes on flows through the same four levels, from the big-picture show down to the individual task. Because the levels are connected, a change at any level updates everything linked to it: a rate on a project rolls up to the production's profit, a completed task updates its session, a booked session reserves the room.

Productions

The show, series, or campaign. Your top-down portfolio view.
Projects

The deliverables inside a production, where budgets, billable resources, and live P&L live.
Events / Sessions

The scheduled units of work, shoots, recordings, and edit sessions, with work orders, talent, and media attached.
Tasks

The assignments that move each session forward, with owners and due dates.

Project Management with Live Profitability Tracking

The Projects level is your command center for every deliverable, and the one place most studio tools leave a gap. It combines scheduling and task tracking with real production budgeting: every project carries live profit and loss, with budgets, billable resources, rates, expenses, and margins updating as the work happens.   • Client, PO, and Invoice Status. Client details, purchase orders, and billing data sit on the project, and you can see at a glance which projects are approved, pending, or billed, with budgets, expenses, and invoices in sync.
  • Master Project List with Smart Filters. Every active project in one sortable view, filterable by client, production, approval, invoice status, project type, or due date, so no deadline or pending bill slips.
  • Tasks and Talent in One Record. Switch between resources, tasks, and talent inside each project, without leaving the project.
  • Profit Analysis and Reporting. Generate real-time P&L summaries and billing reports. Export performance data for finance teams or executives across every active project.

Level 3: Events & Sessions

Session Management with Work Orders, Talent, and Media

Every shoot, recording, and edit session lives in a single connected record. Open a session and everything about it is one click away, financials, work orders, the crew, the media, and the paperwork, so the people running the day never chase details across separate tools.

  • Multi-Tab Session Record. Details, Financials, Work Orders, Tasks, Talent, Media, Files, and Reports, organized as tabs on one screen, so the whole session is accessible at a glance.
  • Session-Level Financials. Live charge, expense, and profit and loss columns update per session, so a single shoot's costs and margin are visible alongside the work itself.
  • Work Order Management. Generate call sheets and gear pull lists in seconds. Linked resource bookings appear under Related Events, keeping rooms, crew, and gear synchronized for airtight logistics.
  • Task and Talent Grid. Assign and update work on a drag-and-drop board, and manage the session's talent, roles, contact details, start and end times, and call sheet flags, in one grid.
  • Media and Reporting Hub. Attach scripts, rough cuts, or Pro Tools sessions directly to the record, and print PDF or CSV usage and itemized reports from the Reports tab for clean documentation and delivery.
