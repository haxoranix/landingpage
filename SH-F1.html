How Studio Hero Structures Your Work

Every production your studio takes on flows through the same four levels, from the big-picture show down to the individual task. Because the levels are connected, a change at any level updates everything linked to it: a rate on a project rolls up to the production's profit, a completed task updates its session, a booked session reserves the room.

Productions

The show, series, or campaign. Your top-down portfolio view.
Projects

The deliverables inside a production, where budgets, billable resources, and live P&L live.
Events / Sessions

The scheduled units of work, shoots, recordings, and edit sessions, with work orders, talent, and media attached.
Tasks

The assignments that move each session forward, with owners and due dates.

Every shoot, recording, and edit session lives in a single connected record. Open a session and everything about it is one click away—financials, work orders, the crew, the media, and the paperwork—so the people running the day never chase details across separate tools.

  • Multi-Tab Session Record. Details, Financials, Work Orders, Tasks, Talent, Media, Files, and Reports, organized as tabs on one screen.
  • Session-Level Financials. Live charge, expense, and profit and loss columns update per session, so a single shoot's costs are visible alongside the work.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Studio Hero different from generic project management tools?
Generic tools like Asana or Monday track tasks but know nothing about rooms, crew, gear, or whether a production made money. Studio Hero is built for studios: it connects production project management to live profit and loss, scheduling, equipment, and invoicing in one system. You manage the work and see the margin in the same place. The fastest way to see the difference is a quick demo on your own workflow.
Can Studio Hero manage multiple shows or productions at once?
Yes. The Productions level is built for multi-project studios, giving producers and executives a top-down portfolio view of every show, series, and campaign, with status, budgets, and progress updating in real time. You can drill from the portfolio down to a single project or session in a click.
How does Studio Hero help me track profitability?
Every project carries live profit and loss.
